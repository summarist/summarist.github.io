Proverbs of David

A selection of proverbs taken from the Book of Psalms in the Hebrew Bible. Verse references such as 12:8 indicate the original source of the text, but donʼt imply a full quotation.

2:11 Serve God with fear and re­joice with trembling.

4:4 In your an­ger, do not sin. Pon­der in your heart on your bed and be still. 5 Of­fer the sac­ri­fices of right­eous­ness and put your trust in God.

12:8 The wick­ed walk on eve­ry side when vile­ness is ex­alt­ed a­mong the chil­dren of men.

15:1 God, who may so­journ in your tent? Who may dwell on your ho­ly hill? 2 He who walks with in­teg­ri­ty and does what is right and speaks truth in his heart; 3 who does not slan­der with his tongue and does no e­vil to his neigh­bor and does not take up a re­proach a­gainst his friend; 4 who des­pises a vile per­son but hon­ors those who fear God; who keeps a prom­ise e­ven when it hurts and does not change; 5 who does not loan out his mon­ey with in­ter­est and does not take a bribe a­gainst the in­no­cent. He who does these things shall nev­er be moved.

18:25 God shows mer­cy to the mer­ci­ful.

18:30 The word of God proves true. He is a shield to all who take ref­uge in him.

19:1 The heav­ens de­clare the glo­ry of God and the skies show his hand­i­work.

19:7 The law of God is per­fect, re­viv­ing the soul. The tes­ti­mo­ny of God is trust­wor­thy, mak­ing wise the sim­ple. 8 The pre­cepts of God are right, re­joic­ing the heart. The com­mand­ment of God is pure, enlightening the eyes. 9 The fear of God is clean, en­dur­ing for ev­er. The laws of God are true and right­eous al­to­geth­er.

27:14 Wait for God. Be strong and let your heart take cour­age. Wait for God.

30:5 Weep­ing may last for a night, but joy co­mes in the morn­ing.

32:10 Man­y sor­rows come to the wick­ed, but lov­ing-kindness sur­rounds him who trusts in God.

33:6 By the word of God the heav­ens were made and all their host by the breath of his mouth.

34:13 Keep your tongue from e­vil and your lips from speak­ing de­ceit. 14 De­part from e­vil and do good. Seek peace and pur­sue it.

37:1 Fret not be­cause of e­vil­do­ers, and en­vy not wrong­do­ers.

37:7 Be still be­fore God and wait pa­tient­ly for him. Fret not be­cause of him who pros­pers in his way; be­cause of him who carries out wick­ed schemes. 8 Cease from an­ger and for­sake wrath. Fret not—it leads on­ly to e­vil­do­ing.

37:16 A lit­tle that the right­eous has is bet­ter than the a­bun­dance of man­y wick­ed.

37:21 The wick­ed bor­rows and does not pay back, but the right­eous is gen­er­ous and gives.

39:1 I sa­id, I will guard my ways so that I may not sin with my tongue. I will muz­zle my mouth while the wick­ed are in my pres­ence.

41:1 Bless­ed is he who con­sid­ers the poor. God will de­liv­er him in the day of trou­ble.

46:1 God is our ref­uge and strength, a ver­y pre­sent help in trou­ble.

46:10 Be still and know that I am God.

49:16 Do not be a­fraid when one is made rich, when the splen­dor of his house in­creases. 17 For when he dies he will car­ry noth­ing a­way. His glo­ry will not de­scend af­ter him.

50:14 Of­fer to God a sac­ri­fice of thanks­giv­ing and pay your vows to the Most High 15 and call on me in the day of trou­ble. I will de­liv­er you and you will glo­ri­fy me.

55:22 Cast your bur­den on God and he will sus­tain you. He will nev­er al­low the right­eous to be moved.

82:3 De­fend the poor and fa­ther­less. Do jus­tice to the af­flicted and des­ti­tute. 4 Res­cue the poor and need­y. De­liv­er them from the hand of the wick­ed.

92:12 The right­eous will flour­ish like the palm tree. He will grow like a ce­dar in Lebanon.

96:2 Sing to God, bless his name. Pro­claim his sal­va­tion from day to day. 3 De­clare his glo­ry a­mong the na­tions, his mar­vel­lous deeds a­mong all the peo­ples.

100:1 Make a joy­ful noise to God, all the earth. 2 Serve God with glad­ness. Come be­fore him with sing­ing.

102:17 God re­gards the pray­er of the des­ti­tute and does not de­spise their pray­er.

103:8 God is mer­ci­ful and gra­cious, slow to an­ger, abounding in lov­ing-kindness.

105:1 Oh give thanks to God. Call on his name. Make known his deeds a­mong the peo­ples. 2 Sing to him. Sing praises to him. Talk a­bout all his mar­vel­ous deeds. 3 Glo­ry in his ho­ly name. Let the hearts of those who seek God re­joice. 4 Seek God and his strength. Seek his face con­tin­u­al­ly. 5 Re­mem­ber the won­ders that he has done, his mir­a­cles and the judg­ments he has pro­nounced.

106:1 Praise God. Oh give thanks to God, for he is good; for his lov­ing-kindness en­dures for­ev­er.

111:10 The fear of God is the be­gin­ning of wis­dom. Those who o­bey his com­mand­ments have good un­der­stand­ing.

112:1 Bless­ed is the man who fears God, who de­lights great­ly in his com­mand­ments.

112:4 For the up­right, light a­rises in the dark­ness. He is gra­cious, mer­ci­ful and right­eous. 5 It is well with the man who deals gen­er­ous­ly and lends, who con­ducts his af­fairs with jus­tice.

118:8 It is bet­ter to take ref­uge in God than to put con­fi­dence in man. 9 It is bet­ter to take ref­uge in God than to put con­fi­dence in princes.

122:6 Pray for the peace of Jerusalem.

127:1 Ex­cept God build the house, they la­bor in vain who build it. Ex­cept God keep the cit­y, the watch­man stays a­wake in vain. 2 It is vain for you to rise up ear­ly, to stay up late, to eat the bread of toil—for he gives sleep to his be­lov­ed.

127:3 Be­hold, chil­dren are a her­it­age from God and the fruit of the womb is a re­ward.

133:1 Be­hold, how good and pleas­ant it is for broth­ers to live to­geth­er in u­ni­ty.

136:1 Oh give thanks to God, for he is good. His lov­ing-kindness en­dures for ev­er.

146:3 Do not put your trust in princes or in the son of man, who can­not save.
